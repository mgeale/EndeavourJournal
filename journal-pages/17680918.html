Sunday 18 Srly. ESE SW ----- ----- ----- ----- ------

Do Weather. PM Ricieved on board 270 pounds of fresh Beef, and a Live Bullock charg'd 613 pounds. Compleated our Wine and Water having rec'd of the former 3032 Gallns of the Latter 10 Tuns. AM unmoor'd and prepar'd for sailing. Funchall, in the Island of Madeira, by Observations made here by D.r Eberton Heberden F.R.S. lies in the Latd of 32° 33' 33" N. & longd West from Greenwich 16° 49' - the Variation of the Compass 15° 30' West decreasing, as he says which I much doubt, neither does this Variation agree ^ with our own observations. The Tide flowes full, and Change North and South and rises perpendicular 7 feet at spring Tides and 4 feet at Neep-tides. We found the North point of the Dipping Needle, belonging to the Royal Society to Dip 77° 18'. The refreshments for shipping to be got at this place are Wine, Water, Fruit of several sorts, and Onions in plenty, some sweat meats; but fresh meat and Poultry are very dear and not to be had at any rate without Leave from the Governor.
